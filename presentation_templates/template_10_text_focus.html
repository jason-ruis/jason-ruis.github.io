Chapter Two

The Question of Authority

What serves as your ultimate standard for determining truth, establishing morality, and discovering meaning in existence?

This fundamental choice becomes the lens through which we interpret all of reality, shaping our understanding of knowledge, ethics, and purpose.

Chapter Three

Essential Inquiries

  • How do we determine what is ultimately true and reliable?
  • What methods lead to genuine knowledge and understanding?
  • Where do moral standards find their foundation and authority?
  • What provides authentic meaning and purpose in life?
  • How do we evaluate competing claims about reality?

These questions reveal our fundamental presuppositions about the nature of reality itself.

Chapter Four

Two Philosophical Approaches

The Path of Autonomous Reason

Human intellect serves as the final arbiter of truth. Knowledge derives primarily from empirical observation and rational analysis. Moral standards emerge from human consensus or individual preference. Meaning is constructed through personal choice and cultural agreement.

The Path of Divine Revelation

Scripture serves as the ultimate authority for truth and knowledge. Divine revelation provides the foundation for understanding reality. God's moral law establishes absolute ethical standards. Meaning flows from divinely ordained purpose and calling.
