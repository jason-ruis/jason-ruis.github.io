The Foundation Question
Lecture 2

The Question of Ultimate Authority

Every rational being must establish a foundational epistemological commitment that serves as their ultimate criterion for determining truth, moral obligation, and existential meaning.

This fundamental choice constitutes what philosophers term one's "ultimate concern" or "basic presupposition," functioning as the interpretive framework through which all subsequent knowledge claims and value judgments are evaluated.

Thesis: Our choice of ultimate authority determines the coherence and consistency of our entire worldview system.

Epistemological Inquiries
Lecture 3

Fundamental Epistemological Questions

  • Ontological: What constitutes the nature of ultimate reality?
  • Epistemological: How do we acquire reliable knowledge about reality?
  • Axiological: What establishes the foundation for moral value systems?
  • Teleological: What provides ultimate purpose and meaning in existence?
  • Methodological: How do we evaluate competing truth claims and worldviews?
These inquiries reveal our fundamental presuppositions about the nature of knowledge, reality, and rational discourse itself.
Comparative Analysis
Lecture 4

Comparative Epistemological Frameworks

Anthropocentric Rationalism
  • Human reason as epistemological foundation
  • Empirical methodology as primary tool
  • Autonomous moral reasoning
  • Existential self-determination

Methodological starting point: Human cognitive autonomy

Theocentric Revelationalism
  • Divine revelation as epistemological foundation
  • Scripture as ultimate authority
  • Theonomous ethical framework
  • Divinely ordained teleology

Methodological starting point: Divine cognitive dependence

Scriptural Foundation
Lecture 5
The fear of the Lord is the beginning of knowledge; fools despise wisdom and instruction.
Proverbs 1:7 (English Standard Version)

The Scriptural assertion establishes that authentic knowledge (Hebrew: da'at) and wisdom (Hebrew: chokmah) find their epistemological foundation in the reverential acknowledgment of divine sovereignty over all dimensions of reality.

This verse provides the fundamental presuppositional framework for a consistently Christian approach to knowledge and understanding.
Practical Implementation
Lecture 6

Implementing a Biblical Epistemology

The practical application of Scripture as ultimate authority yields significant benefits across multiple domains of human experience and inquiry:

  • Cognitive Domain: Provides a consistent and reliable foundation for truth claims and rational discourse
  • Moral Domain: Establishes absolute ethical standards transcending cultural relativism
  • Existential Domain: Offers ultimate meaning and purpose grounded in divine intention
  • Apologetic Domain: Enables confident engagement with competing worldview systems

Research Question: How might these epistemological principles transform contemporary approaches to academic inquiry, ethical reasoning, and existential reflection?

Further study: Examine the implications of presuppositional methodology for interdisciplinary dialogue and scholarly research.
