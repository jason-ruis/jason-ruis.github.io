The Authority Question

1The Question

What serves as your ultimate standard for determining truth, morality, and meaning in life?

2The Impact

This foundational choice shapes our interpretation of all reality and influences every decision we make.

3The Stakes

Our choice determines how we approach knowledge, ethics, meaning, and our understanding of truth itself.

4The Challenge

Everyone operates from some ultimate authority, whether consciously chosen or unconsciously assumed.

Essential Questions to Consider

Truth

  • What determines absolute truth?
  • How do we distinguish truth from error?
  • Can truth be known with certainty?

Knowledge

  • How do we acquire genuine knowledge?
  • What are reliable sources of information?
  • How do we test knowledge claims?

Morality

  • What establishes right and wrong?
  • Are moral standards absolute or relative?
  • Who determines ethical principles?

Meaning

  • What provides ultimate purpose?
  • How do we find significance?
  • What makes life worth living?

Two Foundational Approaches

Human Autonomy

  • Reason as ultimate judge
  • Empirical observation primary
  • Self-determined moral standards
  • Individual construction of meaning

Divine Authority

  • Scripture as ultimate standard
  • Divine revelation foundational
  • God's moral law as guide
  • Divinely ordained purpose

Starting Point: Independence

Begins with human ability to determine truth through reason and experience, making mankind the measure of all things.

Starting Point: Dependence

Begins with God's revelation as the foundation for truth, making Scripture the standard for all knowledge and ethics.
