About This Book

With Heart and Mouth stands as the most comprehensive modern exposition of the Belgic Confession available in English. Daniel R. Hyde has produced a monumental work that combines meticulous historical research, thorough biblical exegesis, and pastoral wisdom to illumine every article of this foundational Reformed confession. At over 650 pages, this volume represents years of careful study and reflection on what has been called "the most beautiful of the Reformed confessions."

The Belgic Confession, written by Guido de Brès in 1561 during a time of severe persecution in the Low Countries, articulates Reformed doctrine in 37 articles covering the full scope of Christian theology—from the knowledge of God to the final judgment. Hyde's exposition honors the confession's original context while demonstrating its enduring relevance for contemporary believers. He carefully traces the historical circumstances that gave birth to each article, showing how the confession emerged from the crucible of persecution and theological controversy.

What makes this work exceptional is Hyde's ability to weave together multiple strands of theological reflection. He provides extensive historical background on the Belgic Confession's composition and reception, examines the biblical foundations for each article, engages with the broader Reformed theological tradition, and offers practical applications for church life today. His writing is both scholarly and accessible, making complex theological concepts understandable without sacrificing depth or precision.

Hyde writes as both a historian and a pastor, someone who loves the Reformed confessions not merely as historical documents but as living expressions of biblical faith. Throughout the book, he demonstrates how the Belgic Confession speaks to perennial questions about God, humanity, salvation, the church, and the Christian life. Whether you're a pastor preparing to teach the confession, a church member seeking to understand your confessional heritage, or a student of Reformed theology, this comprehensive exposition provides an invaluable resource for engaging deeply with one of the church's great confessional statements.

About the Author

Daniel R. Hyde, PhD

Daniel R. Hyde is Senior Pastor of Oceanside United Reformed Church in Carlsbad/Oceanside, California, where he has served since 2004. He is a recognized expert on Reformed liturgy, confessional theology, and church history, with a particular focus on the Dutch Reformed tradition.

Dr. Hyde earned his BA from California State University, his MDiv from Westminster Seminary California, and his PhD from Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam, where he studied Dutch Reformed theology and church history. His doctoral dissertation examined the theology of Guido de Brès, the primary author of the Belgic Confession, making him uniquely qualified to write this comprehensive exposition.

As a prolific author, Hyde has written extensively on Reformed worship, the sacraments, and confessional theology. His other works include God in Our Midst: The Tabernacle and Our Relationship with God, Jesus Loves the Little Children (on infant baptism), and Welcome to a Reformed Church. He is also the editor of the book series Bibles of the Reformation for Reformation Heritage Books.

Dr. Hyde is passionate about helping contemporary Reformed Christians understand and appreciate their rich confessional and liturgical heritage. He serves on the board of directors for the Heidelberg Reformation Association and is a frequent speaker at Reformed conferences and churches. His writing combines scholarly rigor with pastoral warmth, making complex theological topics accessible to ordinary believers.

He blogs regularly at danielrhyde.com and co-hosts the Sola Worship podcast on Reformed worship. Daniel is married to Karajean, and they have five children. His love for the Reformed confessions is matched by his commitment to helping local churches flourish in their understanding and practice of Reformed faith and worship.
