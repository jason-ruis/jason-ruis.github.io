About This Book

In Saving the Reformation, W. Robert Godfrey provides a masterful exploration of one of the most significant moments in church history—the Synod of Dort (1618-1619) and the confession it produced. Written to commemorate the 400th anniversary of the Synod, this book combines historical narrative, theological analysis, and pastoral application to illuminate why the Canons of Dort remain vitally important for Reformed churches today.

The Synod of Dort was convened to address the Arminian controversy that threatened to divide the Dutch Reformed Church and undermine core Reformation doctrines. Godfrey skillfully narrates the historical background of the controversy, introducing readers to key figures like Jacob Arminius, Franciscus Gomarus, and John of Barneveld. He shows how theological disputes became entangled with political machinations, bringing the young Dutch Republic to the brink of civil war. Through careful historical research and engaging storytelling, Godfrey brings this pivotal moment in church history to life.

What distinguishes this book is Godfrey's emphasis on the pastoral theology embodied in the Canons. Rather than presenting the Canons merely as dry doctrinal propositions, he demonstrates how they were crafted to provide pastoral comfort and assurance to believers. The famous TULIP acronym (Total Depravity, Unconditional Election, Limited Atonement, Irresistible Grace, Perseverance of the Saints) represents not abstract theology but good news for sinners—doctrines designed to magnify God's grace and give believers confidence in their salvation.

Godfrey writes as both a historian and a churchman, someone who understands the practical implications of theological controversy. Throughout the book, he addresses contemporary objections to Reformed theology, shows how the Canons continue to speak to modern pastoral concerns, and calls Reformed churches to embrace their confessional heritage. This is essential reading for anyone seeking to understand the Canons of Dort, the doctrines of grace, or the ongoing relevance of Reformed confessional theology.

About the Author

W. Robert Godfrey, PhD

W. Robert Godfrey is President Emeritus and Professor Emeritus of Church History at Westminster Seminary California in Escondido. He is one of the most respected church historians in the Reformed world, with particular expertise in the Reformation era and Dutch Reformed history.

Dr. Godfrey earned his BA from Stanford University, his MDiv from Westminster Theological Seminary, and his MA and PhD from Stanford University. His doctoral dissertation focused on the English Reformed church in the 17th century, establishing his credentials as a careful historical scholar. Over his distinguished career spanning more than four decades, he has become recognized as a leading authority on Reformed confessional history.

As a founding board member and teaching fellow of Ligonier Ministries, Dr. Godfrey has been instrumental in bringing Reformed theology to a broader evangelical audience. His clear teaching style and ability to make historical theology accessible have made him a beloved figure in Reformed circles. He is featured in numerous Ligonier teaching series and has taught church history to thousands of students throughout his career.

Dr. Godfrey is the author of several important works including John Calvin: Pilgrim and Pastor, Reformation Sketches, An Unexpected Journey (autobiography), and Learning to Love the Psalms. He is also a regular contributor to Tabletalk magazine and has written numerous scholarly articles on Reformation history and theology.

Known for his pastoral heart and love for the church, Dr. Godfrey combines rigorous scholarship with a concern for practical application. He has served as an elder in the United Reformed Churches in North America and regularly preaches in churches. His commitment to Reformed confessional theology is matched by his desire to see that theology faithfully applied in contemporary church life. Dr. Godfrey is married to Mary Ellen, and they have three children and several grandchildren.
