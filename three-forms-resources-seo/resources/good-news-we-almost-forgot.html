About This Book

In The Good News We Almost Forgot, Kevin DeYoung invites readers to rediscover the profound wisdom contained in the Heidelberg Catechism, a 450-year-old confession that remains remarkably relevant to contemporary Christian life. Written with clarity, warmth, and pastoral insight, DeYoung demonstrates how this historic document speaks powerfully to modern questions about sin, salvation, and sanctification.

The Heidelberg Catechism organizes Christian doctrine around three fundamental themes: our misery (guilt), God's redemption (grace), and our response (gratitude). DeYoung skillfully unpacks each of these sections, showing how the catechism presents a comprehensive vision of the Christian faith that is both theologically robust and practically applicable. His explanations are accessible to newcomers while offering fresh insights for those already familiar with Reformed theology.

What sets this book apart is DeYoung's ability to connect historic confessional theology with contemporary Christian experience. He addresses common objections to Reformed doctrine, explains difficult concepts with helpful illustrations, and consistently points readers to the grace of God in Christ. Throughout the book, DeYoung maintains a pastoral tone that is both challenging and encouraging, making complex theological truths understandable without oversimplifying them.

Whether you're new to the Heidelberg Catechism or have studied it for years, DeYoung's commentary offers a fresh, engaging exploration of this foundational Reformed document. His work demonstrates why the catechism has remained a beloved teaching tool across centuries and cultures, and why it continues to speak to the heart of Christian faith today.

About the Author

Kevin DeYoung

Kevin DeYoung is Senior Pastor at Christ Covenant Church in Matthews, North Carolina, and serves as associate professor of systematic theology at Reformed Theological Seminary (Charlotte). He is a council member of The Gospel Coalition and has authored numerous books on theology, Christian living, and church ministry.

DeYoung is known for his ability to make Reformed theology accessible to contemporary audiences while maintaining theological rigor and biblical fidelity. His writing combines scholarly insight with pastoral warmth, addressing both the mind and the heart. He has become one of the most influential voices in contemporary Reformed evangelicalism, particularly among younger pastors and church leaders.

A graduate of Michigan State University (BA), Gordon-Conwell Theological Seminary (MDiv), and the University of Leicester (PhD), DeYoung brings both academic credentials and pastoral experience to his work. His other notable books include The Hole in Our Holiness, Just Do Something, Taking God at His Word, and What Does the Bible Really Teach about Homosexuality?

DeYoung is married to Trisha, and they have nine children. He blogs regularly at The Gospel Coalition and maintains an active speaking ministry, addressing conferences and churches around the world.
