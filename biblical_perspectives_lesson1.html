Biblical Perspectives

Lesson 1: Introduction to Biblical Worldview

Understanding the framework that shapes everything.

Course Objectives

Purpose

To understand how the Christian faith provides a comprehensive framework for understanding all of life.

Goal

To develop a distinctly biblical perspective that shapes how we think about reality, knowledge, and ethics.

Why Worldview Matters

  • Every person operates from a worldview or "story" about reality.
  • It determines how we interpret facts, make decisions, and understand our purpose.
  • There is no such thing as neutral thinking — everyone has presuppositions.

What is a Worldview?

A comprehensive framework for understanding reality. It answers three fundamental questions:

  • 1

    How are things supposed to be? (Creation)

  • 2

    What is the main problem with things as they are? (Fall)

  • 3

    What is the solution and how can it be realized? (Redemption)

The Power of Story

Humans cannot make sense of anything without placing it in a narrative structure. Our "story" about reality shapes our responses to every situation.

If we get the story wrong, our life responses will be wrong.

Competing Worldviews

Four common stories about reality.

Secular Humanism

Reality is material, the problem is ignorance, the solution is education/science.

Marxism

Reality is economic, the problem is class struggle, the solution is revolution.

Individualism

Reality is personal freedom, the problem is oppression, the solution is self-actualization.

Christianity

Reality is relational with God, the problem is sin, the solution is redemption through Christ.

The Biblical Worldview Foundation

Our framework rests on three unshakable pillars.

The Authority of Scripture

  • Scripture is self-attesting and requires no external verification.
  • The Word of God must be presupposed, not proven by human reasoning.
But in your hearts honor Christ the Lord as holy, always being prepared to make a defense to anyone who asks you for a reason for the hope that is in you...
1 PETER 3:15

The Lordship of Christ

His authority extends to every area of reality and thought.

Metaphysical

Christ is Lord over all reality and what exists.

Ethical

Christ is Lord over all moral standards and what is right.

Epistemological

Christ is Lord over all knowledge and what is true.

Total Lordship

This Lordship extends to every area of life, including how we think and argue.

The Created Order

  • God is totally self-sufficient and triune.
  • Everything derives its existence, character, meaning, and purpose from Him.
  • There is a categorical distinction between the Creator and creation.
  • Man is created in God's image with capability and original perfection.

The Problem of Human Autonomy

The attempt to live and think independently of God.

The Fall and Its Consequences

Man attempted to replace God's authority with his own self-sufficiency.

The fall didn't remove our ability to reason, but perverted its direction. Fallen man refuses to be subject to God's authority and makes himself the ultimate judge of truth.

The Impossibility of Neutrality

The Believer

Acknowledges Christ as Lord and interprets all facts through the lens of Scripture.

The Unbeliever

Operates from a false view of reality, suppressing the knowledge of God they possess (Romans 1).

No one can take a neutral stance. All reasoning occurs within a framework of basic commitments.

The Strategy of Rebellion

A Pattern from Genesis 3

  • 1

    Satan questions God's Word ("Did God really say...?").

  • 2

    Satan contradicts God's Word ("You will not surely die.").

  • 3

    The temptation is to determine good and evil independently of God.

This is epistemological rebellion: setting human reason above divine revelation.

Presuppositional Apologetics

Defending the faith by challenging the very foundation of unbelief.

The Method

Since neutrality is impossible, we must argue at the presuppositional level.

Step 1: Internal Critique

Show that non-Christian presuppositions ultimately destroy the very possibility of knowledge, logic, and morality.

Step 2: Positive Case

Demonstrate that only within a Christian worldview can anything be known. We prove Christianity from the impossibility of the contrary.

The Goal of Apologetics

  • Our aim is not merely to win arguments, but to call sinners to repentance.
  • We show that their suppressed knowledge of God is what makes rational discourse possible at all.
  • We must depend on the Holy Spirit to open blinded eyes and soften hardened hearts.

Practical Implications

Applying the Biblical Worldview to All of Life.

For Daily Life

Work, relationships, politics, art, and science must all be evaluated through the lens of Scripture.

For Cultural Engagement

Appreciate the good work of non-Christians (common grace) while recognizing their flawed foundations.

For Christian Living

Hold fast to biblical presuppositions, submitting humbly to Scripture as the final authority.

Reject False Neutrality

We must actively reject the assumption that secular thinking is neutral ground.

Discussion Questions

  • Can you think of a time when your "story" about a situation was wrong, and how that affected your response?
  • What are some examples of how secular worldviews shape contemporary culture's approach to work, relationships, or ethics?
  • How does understanding the impossibility of neutrality change the way we engage with non-Christian friends and colleagues?
  • What are the practical implications of Christ's lordship over our thinking and reasoning?

Key Terms to Remember

Worldview:
A comprehensive framework for understanding reality.

Presupposition:
Basic assumptions we bring to our thinking about any topic.

Autonomy:
The attempt to think and live independently of God's authority.

Self-attesting:
Scripture's quality of carrying its own evidence and requiring no external proof.

Common Grace:
God's general blessings on all humanity, enabling even unbelievers to access truth and do good work.
